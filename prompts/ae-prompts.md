## Call Preparation

### Full Discovery Call Brief
```
Generate my call-prep brief for the discovery call with [Company] at [time] today.

Include:
1. Account summary: company overview, our relationship history, last interaction
2. Prior interactions: emails exchanged, previous calls, open commitments
3. 5 open discovery questions tailored to:
   - Their industry: [industry]
   - Known challenge: [challenge from research or prior conversation]
   - Decision stage: [early / mid / late]
4. MEDDIC template pre-populated with what we already know:
   - Metrics: [known or Unknown]
   - Economic Buyer: [name/title or Unknown]
   - Decision Criteria: [known or Unknown]
   - Decision Process: [known or Unknown]
   - Identify Pain: [known or Unknown]
   - Champion: [name/title or Unknown]
5. 2 likely objections with recommended responses
6. Competitive landscape: who else they may be evaluating and our differentiation
7. 3 talk track bullets specific to their situation

Post to [Company] Notion deal room — "Calls" tab.
```

### Quick 10-Minute Call Prep
```
Quick call prep for [Company] discovery call in 15 minutes.

I need only:
- 3 best discovery questions for a [type: cloud migration / security / analytics / etc.] conversation
- Their most recent news item I can reference to open
- One competitive differentiator if [Competitor] comes up
- The one thing I must learn on this call to advance the deal

Keep it to one page. Speed matters.
```

### Technical Validation Call Prep
```
Generate a technical call-prep brief for [Company] — architecture validation call.

Attendees from their side: [names and titles]
Our SE joining: [SE name]

Prepare:
- Technical environment summary: what we know about their stack
- Open technical questions we need answered (focus on: architecture, scale, integrations, security/compliance)
- Technical objections likely to arise and recommended responses
- Demo or proof points to prepare for this specific stack
- Success criteria for this call: what must we confirm to move to proposal?
```

### Executive Business Review Prep
```
Prepare a QBR / EBR brief for [Company] meeting with [Exec Title].

Context:
- Relationship stage: [active customer / late-stage prospect / renewal]
- Their business goals this year: [goals from prior conversations or public sources]
- What we've delivered: [outcomes, metrics, milestones]
- Open risks or concerns: [known issues]

Prepare:
- 5-slide narrative arc (executive summary, their goals, our contribution, proof points, next 90 days)
- 3 strategic discussion questions for the exec
- Any competitive intelligence on alternatives they may be considering
- Recommended ask / next step from this meeting
```

## Post-Call Follow-Up

### Full Call Summary (/call-summary)
```
/call-summary [Company] — [call type: discovery / technical validation / negotiation / close]
Date: [date]
Transcript: [attach or paste Fireflies/Gong link]

Extract:
1. Buyer's pain in their exact words (direct quotes from transcript)
2. Economic buyer: name, title, identified Y/N
3. Decision criteria stated explicitly by prospect
4. Decision process: steps, timeline, who else is involved
5. Competitors mentioned (named or implied)
6. Champion identified: Y/N, name, title
7. Next steps agreed on-call — specific, with dates and owners
8. My action items with due dates
9. MEDDIC gap analysis: which fields still unknown?
10. Deal health: Improving / Stable / At Risk (with 1-line rationale)

Post summary to [Company] Notion deal room — "Calls" tab.
Draft follow-up email for my review (under 150 words, don't send yet).
Recommend CRM updates: stage, close date, deal value, contacts to add.
```

### Follow-Up Email (Post-Discovery)
```
Draft a follow-up email to [Name] at [Company] after our discovery call today.

Key points to include:
- Thank them for their time (one sentence, genuine not generic)
- Summarize the 2-3 key pain points they shared (in their language, not ours)
- Confirm next steps agreed on the call: [step 1, step 2, step 3]
- Any resources promised (attach or link): [resource]
- Soft confirmation of the next meeting if scheduled

Tone: warm but professional. Max 150 words. No "please feel free to reach out."
```

### Action Item Confirmation
```
Draft a brief action item recap email to [Name] and [other attendees] at [Company].

After our call today, I wanted to confirm the action items we agreed on:

Items from their side:
- [item 1 — owner — due date]
- [item 2 — owner — due date]

Items from our side:
- [item 1 — owner — due date]
- [item 2 — owner — due date]

Next meeting: [date/time or TBD]

Keep it short and structured — this is a logistics email, not a relationship email.
Under 100 words plus the bullet lists.
```

## Proposal & Negotiation

### Proposal Request (create-an-asset)
```
Create a proposal draft for [Company].

Deal context:
- Need: [specific problem or project]
- Timeline: [customer's timeline]
- Budget signal: [range or "not discussed"]
- Primary concern: [their stated #1 concern in their words]
- Economic buyer: [name, title]
- Champion: [name, title]
- Competitor in evaluation: [competitor or "none known"]
- Our differentiator for this deal: [specific to their situation]

Use our [service name] proposal template from Notion.
Include 3 engagement tiers: [Tier 1 name] / [Tier 2 name] / [Tier 3 name].
Tailor the executive summary to their stated pain: "[exact quote from discovery call]"
Highlight [specific differentiator] as the primary reason to choose us.

Post draft to [Company] Notion deal room — "Proposals" tab.
```

### Competitive Response
```
[Company] mentioned they're evaluating [Competitor Name] alongside us.

Prepare a competitive response:
1. Top 3 differentiators vs. [Competitor] for [this type of deal]
   — frame from buyer's perspective (what they care about), not ours
2. Questions I should ask to surface where [Competitor] falls short
3. Any red flags or risks with [Competitor] I can introduce (factual only, no FUD)
4. Talk track for "why us over [Competitor]" (60 seconds, conversational)
5. References or case studies where we displaced [Competitor]

Keep competitive claims factual and defensible.
```

### Pricing Objection Response
```
[Name] at [Company] pushed back on our pricing, saying: "[exact objection in their words]"

Draft a response strategy:
1. Acknowledge their concern without immediately discounting
2. Reframe value: what is the cost of NOT solving this problem?
3. Options to present: [full price / phased approach / smaller scope to start]
4. If we must discount: structure it as a concession with a reason (not just a cave)
5. Next step to propose that keeps the deal moving

Also draft the actual email/message response to their pricing pushback.
Max 150 words. No desperation.
```

## Pipeline Management & Forecasting

### Deal Health Check
```
Review the current state of my deal with [Company].

CRM data:
- Stage: [stage]
- Close date: [date]
- Deal value: $[amount]
- Days in current stage: [N]
- Last activity: [date and type]

MEDDIC status: [paste known fields]

Assess:
1. Deal health: Green / Yellow / Red (with rationale)
2. Biggest risk to this deal closing
3. Top 2 actions I should take in the next 7 days
4. Is the close date realistic? If not, what needs to happen to hit it?
```

### Re-Engagement of Stalled Deal
```
My deal with [Company] has stalled. Details:
- Stage: [stage]
- Last interaction: [N] days ago
- Last communication: [brief description]
- What I know about their internal situation: [any context]

Draft a re-engagement strategy:
1. Most likely reason for silence (with probability estimate)
2. New trigger event or insight I can use to re-engage: [search for one]
3. Re-engagement email draft (under 100 words, not desperate)
4. Alternative: who else at [Company] could I reach out to if this contact is stuck?

Also check: is there any recent news about [Company] I can reference?
```

### Weekly Forecast Update
```
/forecast Update my weekly forecast for [week ending date].

My open pipeline:
[Paste deal list or describe: Company / Stage / Value / Close Date / Confidence %]

My quota: $[amount] for [Q/month]
Closed so far this period: $[amount]

Generate:
- Weighted forecast (use my confidence %s)
- Best case / Most likely / Worst case scenarios
- Gap to quota in each scenario
- Top 3 deals most likely to close this period and recommended actions for each
- 1 deal I should deprioritize based on deal health signals
```
